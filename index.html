Overcome Your Hot Mess Embrace Your Inner Megastar

Every woman carries a broken version of herself—unseen, unappreciated, unloved. This book dives headfirst into that reality, taking you through the amusing anecdote of one woman’s journey: how she dusted off her crown, set it right and said, ‘God made me queen—deal with it!’ Jhute Malinao, with her signature humor, honesty and heart, courageously tells her story - a life shadowed by insecurities and sprinkled with moments of quiet desperation. Convinced she was running low on beauty, opportunities, and dignity, she navigated her darkest days. Yet, through it all, she rose—her cracks allowing God’s light to shine through. If you have ever been trapped in the messiness of life, let this book whisper truths you have longed to hear. This is a love letter to your soul, a mirror reflecting the grace you have always carried. Bring home this treasure to flip your script, grab your crown, and to reclaim the royal spotlight that has always been yours. More than a memoir, this is your very own journal, too. Between its pages are blank spaces waiting for your unique stories of heartbreak, hope, and healing.

About the Author

About the author: BABAE. Madalas Maganda, Minsan Ma-Eme. (Overcome Your Hot Mess, Embrace Your Inner Megastar) is her debut masterpiece. Currently, she is the Head Teacher for Senior High School at Colegio San Agustin Makati, after 15 years as an Academic Coordinator. She teaches Grade 12 Media and Information Literacy and loves every minute of it. She holds a Bachelor of Science in Guidance and Counseling from Sta. Isabel College Manila, a Master of Arts in Education specializing in Religious Education and Values Education from De La Salle University, Manila, and a Master of Arts in Media Literacy Education from the Pauline Institute of Communications in Asia. Jhute lives in a cozy home in Parañaque, she helps raise three nephews, four nieces, and three grandchildren and another one on the way. Single but ready to mingle (ITR required upon introduction), she cannot wake up not teaching and speaking. A self-taught doodler and a trying hard sketcher, Jhute is an advocate of journal writing and finds joy in cold weather, quiet restaurants, long conversations, buffalo wings, Mango Bravo, pistachio nuts, and oddly satisfying videos of grass cutting and carpet cleaning. She is also a binge-watcher of long-running hospital dramas, courtroom sagas, and house makeover shows—the perfect blend of intense emotions and inspiring transformations.She finds delight in cathartic Aegis songs and intense Air Supply ballads. A convert to Koreanovelas, she will marry Lee Min Ho, Song Joong-Ki, or Kim Seon-Ho in a heartbeat. A no-show at most parties and allergic to traffic and commuting, she hoards art and writing materials like treasure. Though she battles hair fall shampoos and arthritis, Jhute’s humor and zest for life remain untouched—a true megastar in her own right.
